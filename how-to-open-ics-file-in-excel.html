How to Open ICS File in Excel (2025 Guide)

Published: April 2025 | Updated: April 2025

Microsoft Excel cannot open ICS files directly. To open an ICS calendar file in Excel you must first convert it to CSV format, then open the CSV in Excel. This guide walks you through every step.

Step 1 — Export Your Calendar as ICS

First export your calendar as an ICS file. In Google Calendar, go to Settings, click Import & Export in the left sidebar, then click Export. You will receive a ZIP file — extract it to get the .ics file.

Step 2 — Convert ICS to CSV

Excel does not support the ICS format natively. Use a free online ICS to CSV converter tool to transform your calendar file into a format Excel can read. Upload your .ics file and download the converted CSV output.

Step 3 — Open CSV in Excel

Open Microsoft Excel. Click File → Open and navigate to your converted CSV file. Excel will automatically detect the comma-separated values and split the data into columns including event title, start date, end date, location, and description.

Step 4 — Format the Data

After importing, select the date columns and apply Excel date formatting so the dates display correctly. You can then use Excel filters, sorting, and pivot tables to analyze your calendar events by date range, category, or any other field.

Why Open ICS in Excel?

Opening calendar data in Excel lets you create custom reports, track time spent on events, filter by date range, and visualize your schedule using Excel charts and pivot tables.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Excel open ICS files directly?

No. Excel does not have native ICS support. You must convert ICS to CSV or XLSX first before opening in Excel.

Will I lose data converting ICS to CSV?

Basic event fields like title, date, time, and description are preserved. Complex fields like recurring event rules may not convert perfectly.
