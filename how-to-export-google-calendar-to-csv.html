How to Export Google Calendar Events to CSV (2025)

Published: April 2025 | Updated: April 2025 | Author: Tech Guides Team

Exporting Google Calendar events to CSV is useful for backups, data analysis, reporting, or migrating to another calendar service. Google Calendar does not export directly to CSV — it exports to ICS format first, which you then convert to CSV. This guide covers the complete process.

Why Export Google Calendar to CSV?

CSV files are universally supported by spreadsheet tools including Microsoft Excel, Google Sheets, LibreOffice Calc, and virtually every data analysis platform. Exporting your calendar to CSV lets you:

  • Create custom reports on your events by date, category, or duration
  • Back up your calendar data in a format that does not depend on Google
  • Import your calendar events into project management tools or CRMs
  • Analyze time spent on meetings, tasks, and events using pivot tables
  • Share calendar data with colleagues who do not use Google Calendar

Step 1 — Open Google Calendar Settings

Open your browser and go to calendar.google.com. Make sure you are signed into the Google account whose calendar you want to export. Click the gear icon in the top right corner of the page. A dropdown menu will appear — select Settings from the list.

This opens the Google Calendar Settings page where you can manage all your calendar preferences, integrations, and data import/export options.

Step 2 — Navigate to Import and Export

In the left sidebar of the Settings page, scroll down until you see the Import & Export option. Click it to expand the import and export section. You will see two options: Import (for adding calendar data to your account) and Export (for downloading your calendar data).

Step 3 — Export Your Calendar

Under the Export section, click the Export button. Google will immediately generate a ZIP file containing your calendar data and download it to your device. The ZIP file contains one .ics file for each calendar in your Google account.

If you have multiple calendars — for example a personal calendar, a work calendar, and a birthdays calendar — each will have its own separate .ics file inside the ZIP. Extract the ZIP file to access the individual ICS files.

Step 4 — Convert ICS to CSV

Google Calendar exports in ICS (iCalendar) format, not CSV. To open your calendar data in Excel or Google Sheets you need to convert the ICS file to CSV. Use a free online ICS to CSV converter tool — upload your .ics file and download the converted CSV output.

The CSV file will contain one row per calendar event with columns for event title, start date, start time, end date, end time, location, and description. You can then open this CSV directly in Excel or import it into Google Sheets.

Exporting a Specific Calendar Only

The Export button in Google Calendar Settings exports ALL your calendars at once as a ZIP file. If you only want to export a specific calendar, there is an alternative method using the individual calendar settings.

In Google Calendar, find the calendar you want to export in the left sidebar. Click the three dots next to the calendar name and select Settings and sharing. Scroll down to the Integrate calendar section. Copy the Secret address in iCal format URL. Paste this URL in your browser to download just that calendar as an ICS file, then convert it to CSV.

Exporting Google Calendar on Mobile

The Google Calendar mobile app for Android and iOS does not include a built-in export feature. To export from mobile, open a browser on your phone and go to calendar.google.com — the desktop site has the export option available. Alternatively, use a desktop or laptop computer to perform the export.

Common Issues

Export Button Downloads an Empty ZIP

If the exported ZIP file is empty or contains only a small ICS file with no events, check that your calendar actually contains events and that you are signed into the correct Google account.

ICS File Won't Open in Excel

Excel cannot open ICS files directly. You must convert the ICS file to CSV format first. See our guide on how to convert ICS to CSV without losing data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Google Calendar export directly to CSV?

No. Google Calendar only exports in ICS (iCalendar) format. To get a CSV file you must first export the ICS file and then convert it using a free online converter tool.

How often should I export my Google Calendar?

For backup purposes, exporting monthly is sufficient for most users. If you rely on your calendar for business records, consider exporting weekly and storing the CSV files in a secure location.

Can I import the CSV back into Google Calendar?

No. Google Calendar only supports ICS format for imports, not CSV. Keep the original ICS file if you need to re-import your calendar data later.
