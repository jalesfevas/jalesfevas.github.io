How to Convert ICS to CSV on Windows 10 (2025)

Published: April 2025 | Updated: April 2025

Converting ICS to CSV on Windows 10 does not require installing any software. You can use a free online converter tool directly in your browser. This guide covers the exact steps.

Step 1 — Locate Your ICS File

Find your ICS file on Windows 10. If you exported from Google Calendar, check your Downloads folder for a ZIP file. Right-click the ZIP and select Extract All to get the .ics file inside.

Step 2 — Open a Free ICS to CSV Converter

Open Chrome, Edge, or Firefox on Windows 10 and navigate to a free online ICS to CSV converter tool. No software installation is needed — the conversion runs entirely in your browser.

Step 3 — Upload and Convert the ICS File

Click the Upload or Choose File button on the converter. Select your .ics file from Windows Explorer. The tool will automatically parse all calendar events and map them to CSV columns including SUMMARY, DTSTART, DTEND, LOCATION, and DESCRIPTION fields.

Step 4 — Download and Open the CSV File

Click Download CSV to save the converted file to your Windows 10 PC. Open it with Microsoft Excel — Excel will automatically detect the CSV format and display your calendar events in rows and columns.

Alternative Method — Using PowerShell on Windows 10

Advanced users can convert ICS to CSV using Windows 10 PowerShell with a custom script. This method gives you full control over which ICS fields to include in the CSV output and works offline without an internet connection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to upload ICS files to an online converter?

Your ICS file contains calendar event data. Use a reputable converter tool and avoid uploading files containing sensitive meeting information to unknown services.

Does Windows 10 have a built-in ICS to CSV converter?

No. Windows 10 does not include a native ICS to CSV conversion tool. You need either an online converter or a third-party application.
